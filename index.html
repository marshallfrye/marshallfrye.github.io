Research Focus

Engineering
Polar Materials

I work at the intersection of synthesis, characterization, and phase-field simulation to understand what governs ferroelectric behavior at the atomic level — and how to use that understanding to build better materials for memory and energy applications.

01

Interface and Defect Engineering

Oxygen vacancies and interfacial charge do not just degrade devices. They control how polarization switches. In Hf0.5Zr0.5O2, I have mapped how defect populations set the internal bias, coercive field, and remanent polarization.

02

2D Chalcogenide Ferroelectrics

SnSe is a van der Waals material with predicted emergent electronic and structural changes as the 2D limit is approached. I developed wafer-scale PLD deposition with monolayer precision and reported the first bulk P-E and PUND measurements on this system.

03

Rare-Earth Hexagonal Ferrites

Hexagonal ferrites have unique potential for multiferroic and polar photovoltaic applications, but are hindered by their metastability. I used interfacial engineering to elucidate the stabilization mechanism of ScFeO3 thin films and expanded the approach to conductive substrates needed for devices.

04

Ferroelectric Photovoltaics and Memory

Ferroelectric polarization can store bits and separate light-generated charge carriers at voltages above the bandgap limit. In an invited review in Accounts of Materials Research, I laid out why ferroelectric solar cells have underperformed and what material changes could make them competitive.

Research Vision

Where the
Work is Going

As device thickness shrinks, interfaces and defects increasingly dominate over bulk properties. Historically treated as degradation mechanisms, these effects also open routes to precisely tune material behavior. My goal is a unified framework in which interfaces and defects are tunable parameters governing electronic properties, enabling the stabilization, modulation, and enhancement of ferroelectricity across diverse material systems.

01

Defect-Control in Emerging Ferroelectric Memory

Wake-up, imprint, and fatigue in Wurtzite and fluorite devices trace to defect redistribution under cycling. Mapping the full defect-field-cycling phase space should make reliability modeling for FeRAM and FeFET devices more tractable.

02

Polar Materials for Photodetection

With the right deposition approach, SnSe, ScFeO3, and LuFeO3 combine visible-light band gaps with spontaneous polarization. Systematic interlayer and substrate engineering should push bulk photovoltaic efficiency toward the theoretical ceiling, enabling ultrafast light detection in downscalable devices with above-bandgap photovoltages.

03

Science Consultancy

My training covers synthesis, characterization, modeling, and grant strategy. That combination is useful for advising technology development programs, materials qualification work, or R&D roadmapping in the semiconductor and clean-energy sectors.
